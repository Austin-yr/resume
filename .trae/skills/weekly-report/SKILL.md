# Weekly Report Skill

## Overview
This skill helps create structured weekly reports that effectively communicate progress, achievements, challenges, and future plans.

## Report Structure

### Standard Format
1. **Header**
   - Report Title: "Weekly Report"
   - Period: [Start Date] to [End Date]
   - Reported By: [Your Name]
   - Department/Team: [Your Department]
   - Date Submitted: [Current Date]

2. **Executive Summary**
   - Brief overview of the week's key achievements and challenges
   - 2-3 sentences maximum

3. **Key Achievements**
   - List of significant accomplishments during the week
   - Include metrics and outcomes where possible
   - Focus on impact and results

4. **Ongoing Projects**
   - Status update on current projects
   - Progress percentage or milestone achieved
   - Next steps required

5. **Challenges & Solutions**
   - Obstacles encountered during the week
   - Actions taken to address them
   - Support needed if applicable

6. **Plans for Next Week**
   - Prioritized tasks and goals
   - Deadlines and milestones
   - Resources required

7. **Additional Notes**
   - Any other relevant information
   - Team updates or announcements

## Best Practices

### Content Guidelines
- **Be specific**: Use concrete examples and metrics
- **Focus on results**: Highlight what was accomplished, not just what was done
- **Be concise**: Keep each section focused and to the point
- **Be honest**: Accurately report challenges and setbacks
- **Use bullet points**: Make the report easy to scan

### Writing Tips
- Start with the executive summary to provide context
- Quantify achievements where possible (e.g., "Increased sales by 15%")
- Be consistent in formatting and structure
- Proofread for clarity and accuracy
- Submit on time to maintain reliability

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Being too vague**: Avoid statements like "I worked on various tasks"
- **Including too much detail**: Focus on key points, not every minor activity
- **Neglecting challenges**: Honest reporting helps identify areas for improvement
- **Missing deadlines**: Late reports reduce their effectiveness
- **Forgetting to proofread**: Typos and errors undermine professionalism
